Cell Ring Colors

Empty
Available for use. Queen can lay eggs here, or workers can store food. May need cleaning first if dirty.
Egg
Freshly laid by the queen. Takes ~3 mins to hatch into larvae. Essential for maintaining hive population.
Larvae (Hungry)
Needs feeding! Nurse bees must feed larvae with royal jelly. Unfed larvae can die, reducing hive population.
Larvae (Fed)
Well-fed larvae developing normally. Will soon pupate into capped brood. Critical stage for future workers.
Capped Brood
Sealed pupae transforming into adult bees. Takes ~12 mins. New workers will emerge to help the colony!
Nectar
Raw nectar brought by foragers. Will slowly convert to honey. Used to feed larvae and provide energy for workers.
Honey
Partially processed nectar. Primary food storage for the hive. Consumed slowly by bees to survive, especially through winter.
Capped Honey
Fully matured and sealed honey. Long-term food storage. Workers cap it with wax to preserve it indefinitely.
Pollen
Fresh pollen collected from flowers. Rich in protein, essential for feeding larvae and young bees. Will ferment into bee bread.
Bee Bread
Fermented pollen with added enzymes. More nutritious and easier to digest. Critical for brood development and worker health.
Water
Collected water for cooling the hive and diluting honey. Essential during hot weather to regulate temperature through evaporation.

Bee Dot Colors

Queen
The heart of the colony! Lays all eggs (up to 2,000/day). Lives ~3 years. If she dies, workers create an emergency replacement from larvae.
Young Worker
Younger bees (0-50% lifespan). Work inside: nursing larvae, feeding queen, cleaning cells, attending queen. Critical for brood care.
Old Worker
Older bees (50-100% lifespan). Forage outside, collect water, cap honey, remove dead bees. More experienced but slower. Risk death while foraging.

Cargo Indicators

Carrying Nectar
Bee returning from flowers with nectar. Will store in cells on hive edges. Most common forage item during spring/summer.
Carrying Pollen
Bee carrying protein-rich pollen. Essential for feeding larvae. Stored separately from honey, usually near brood.
Carrying Water
Water collector! Brings water when hive overheats. Used for cooling via evaporation and diluting thick honey for consumption.
Carrying Food
Bee carrying honey or pollen from storage to feed larvae or the queen. Shows internal food distribution. Bees must collect food before nursing.

Special

Entrance
Hive entrance (green ring). Bees use these cells to enter/exit for foraging. Typically at the bottom of the hive. Guarded in real hives.
Dirty Cell (Brown Dot)
This cell needs cleaning. Worker bees will polish and prepare it for reuse. Cells become dirty after brood emerges or food is consumed.
